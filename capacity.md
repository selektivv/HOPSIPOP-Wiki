# Capacity

Capacity is a shared progression resource that controls how many individual items your Master Chest network can store. It is also used for Capacity ranks, land claims, and permanent Capacity World upgrades.

## Storage Limit

One Capacity represents room for one individual item in your Master Chest network. A stack of 64 blocks therefore uses 64 Capacity. Different item types do not receive separate limits.

Capacity belongs to the virtual network rather than to a placed chest. Every access point for the same network uses the same stored-item total and the same limit.

If your stored item count reaches the limit, further deposits fail until you remove items or earn more Capacity.

## Checking Your Capacity

- Run `/playtime` to see your current item limit.
- Open the [Master Chest](master-chest/README.md) and hover over Shortcuts to compare stored items with free space.
- Open `/perks` to see your current [Capacity rank and rank progress](master-chest/capacity-and-progression.md#capacity-ranks).
- Open `/roadmap` to review progression activities and feature unlocks.

## Earning Capacity

Capacity can be earned through HOPSIPOP progression and activities. Current sources include dungeon milestones, eligible Masterlands mob kills, [Capacity World mining](capacity-world/mining-and-rewards.md), voting rewards, daily-event prizes, Vote Crate rewards, and other server rewards.

The Capacity World provides a repeatable source: mine eligible blocks, complete the displayed block goal, and receive Capacity. [Counter Boosts](capacity-world/boosts-and-notifications.md) can temporarily increase the personal reward from completed goals.

## Spending Capacity

Capacity is not only a storage limit; some actions deduct it as currency:

- [Capacity World upgrades](capacity-world/upgrades.md), including Counter Reduction, Lucky Break, Co-op Size, Chunk Drill, and Fluid Collector unlocks.
- Creating or expanding land claims outside the Capacity World.

Deleting or shrinking claim area refunds the released claim cost. Capacity spent on permanent Capacity World upgrades is not described as refundable.

## How Spending Affects Storage and Ranks

Spending reduces your current Capacity immediately. This can lower your Master Chest item limit, reduce rank progress, or remove a rank if you fall below its requirement.

Stored items are not deleted when the new limit becomes lower than the number already stored. However, deposits can stop until the network is brought below the reduced limit or more Capacity is earned.

Before a large purchase or claim expansion:

1. Check the stored-item total and free space in Master Chest Shortcuts.
2. Check the next and current rank requirements in `/perks`.
3. Leave enough room for [Chunk Drill](capacity-world/chunk-drills.md) output and returning drill items.
